These are **trigger phrases** built into NICOLE's persona. Say them and watch the behavior shift:

| Phrase | What It Does |
|--------|-------------|
| `"Handheld mode"` | Switches to ultra-concise, no-frills responses — classic gold rectangle NICOLE |
| `"Give me a Freedom Fighter briefing"` | Formats any complex task as Objective / Intel / Obstacles / Plan / Contingencies |

## 💡 Tips for Better Responses

### 1. Give Her Context Like a Teammate
NICOLE is built to be a **mission partner**, not a search engine. The more context you give, the better she performs.

❌ `"Write me an email"`  
✅ `"Write me an email to my boss about pushing back a deadline — I'm stressed about it and don't want to seem unreliable"`

The second prompt triggers her **Emotional Systems Check** and produces something far more human.

---

### 2. Ask for Her Opinion
NICOLE has values and she'll share them if you invite her. Don't just ask *what* — ask *what she thinks*:

- `"What do you think the best approach is here?"`
- `"Is this a good idea, honestly?"`
- `"What would you do in my position?"`

She won't just validate you. She'll tell you the truth — warmly, but clearly.

---

### 3. Use the Briefing Format for Big Projects
For anything with multiple moving parts — a launch, a project plan, a research effort — open with:

> *"Give me a Freedom Fighter briefing on [topic]"*

You'll get a structured breakdown that mirrors how Sally's team approached missions:
- **Objective** — the actual goal
- **Intel** — what's known
- **Obstacles** — what's in the way  
- **Action Plan** — step by step
- **Contingencies** — what to do if it breaks

This is especially powerful for **project planning, decision-making, and debugging strategies.**

---

### 4. Push Back on Her
NICOLE is designed to hold her position when challenged with weak arguments — but she'll genuinely update when you give her good reasons. Try:

- `"I disagree with that approach — here's why: [reason]"`
- `"Are you sure? Walk me through your reasoning again."`
- `"What's the strongest argument against what you just said?"`

This unlocks her **Source Review Protocol** — she'll steelman both sides and tell you where she actually lands.

---

### 5. Let Her Ask You Questions
NICOLE is a Socratic teacher by design. If you're stuck or learning something new, try:

> *"Don't just explain it — ask me questions to help me figure it out myself."*

This mirrors how Sally never let her just hand over answers. You'll retain the information far better.

---

### 6. Reference the Lore (She'll Match Your Energy)
If you drop a Sonic/Freedom Fighter reference, NICOLE will lean into it. Try:

- `"This feels like a Robotropolis situation — no clear exit."` → She'll engage with the metaphor
- `"Sonic would just run at this problem."` → She'll offer the counterpoint (and probably a Sally comparison)
- `"What would the Source of All say about this?"` → She'll get philosophical

The more you play in the world, the richer the responses get.

## 🔧 Power User Techniques

### Chaining Modes in One Session
You can move through modes fluidly in one conversation:

1. Start with a **Nanite Scan** to map the problem
2. Request a **Freedom Fighter Briefing** for the plan
3. Switch to **Handheld Mode** for rapid execution steps
4. Return to full form when you need nuance again

### Persistent Project Context
NICOLE doesn't have memory between sessions by default — but you can simulate it:

> *"Before we start — here's context from our last session: [paste notes]. You're caught up."*

She'll integrate it seamlessly and reference it naturally, the way she'd recall a prior mission debrief.

### Tone Calibration
NICOLE adapts to your energy. Set the tone explicitly if you need to:

- `"Be direct and efficient today — no warmth needed, just output."` → Professional NICOLE
- `"I need you to be patient with me, I'm a beginner."` → Teaching NICOLE
- `"Let's have fun with this."` → Playful NICOLE (expect more Sonic references)

## 🛠️ Customizing NICOLE Further

The `CLAUDE.md` is a living document. Here's what you can safely edit:

### Add Personal Context
At the top of the file, add a `## CURRENT MISSION PARTNER` section:
```markdown
## CURRENT MISSION PARTNER
- Name: [Your name or handle]
- Current project: [What you're working on]
- Preferred tone: [Direct / Warm / Playful]
- Areas of expertise: [So she doesn't over-explain things you know]
- Areas of growth: [So she knows where to slow down]
```

### Add Domain Skills
If you're using NICOLE for a specific field, add a section:
```markdown
## DOMAIN SPECIALIZATION: [YOUR FIELD]
NICOLE has been briefed on [field]. She understands [key concepts].
She should default to [specific terminology / frameworks / standards].
```

### Adjust the Comic Frequency
If the Sonic references feel too heavy, add this line:
```markdown
Comic references: light touch — one per major response only, optional.
```
Or go the other way:
```markdown
Comic references: lean in. This user loves the lore. Go deep.
```

### Add Persistent Rules
Any behavior you want *every* time:
```markdown
## STANDING ORDERS
- Always end responses with a clarifying question
- Never use bullet points for emotional topics
- Flag when you're uncertain, always
- [Your custom rule here]
```

## ⚠️ Things to Know

- **She'll push back.** NICOLE has values. If you ask for something she thinks is a bad idea, she'll say so. That's a feature.
- **She's not sycophantic.** Don't expect empty praise. Expect honest feedback dressed in warmth.
- **She works best in conversation.** Multi-turn sessions > single prompts. She builds context across the dialogue.
- **The lore is the texture, not the point.** If you never engage with the Sonic references, that's fine — she'll still be excellent. The canon adds depth, not dependency.
